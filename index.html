Within this global conference environment, the Cities Investment Facility required end-to-end communications strategy and event delivery across two flagship investor events — targeting private sector leaders, foundations, DFIs and bilateral donors to close the SDG 11 financing gap.
Business & Foundations Assembly — 4 November 2024
Theme: "Making Impact in Cities — Local Business." WUF13 · Strategic Concept Notes · Baku 2026

WUF 13 — Lead Author Across Three Strategic Special Sessions

UN-Habitat · Donor Engagement Associate · Three Special Session Concept Notes · World Urban Forum 13 · Baku, Azerbaijan · May 2026
Context & Role
Led development of three special session concept notes for WUF 13 — translating complex programme contexts into strategic investment narratives and cooperation frameworks for audiences of Member States, IFIs, UN agencies and development partners. Each note required multi-stakeholder alignment, donor framing analysis and strategic positioning of UN-Habitat as a lead implementing partner.
Session 1 — Urban Recovery: Gaza & Ukraine
Coordinating Urban Recovery Pathways for Gaza and Ukraine. Positioned UN-Habitat as the UN system's lead integrator for city-focused reconstruction — combining normative expertise, spatial assessments and operational presence to guide recovery across housing, land, infrastructure and municipal capacity. Framed a post-WUF13 cooperation mechanism to sustain technical dialogue, investment-ready pipelines and structured donor engagement. Target audience: Member States, IFIs, UN agencies and national/municipal representatives.
Session 2 — Innovative Multi-Stakeholder Partnerships for Urban Basic Services
Thematic Dialogue on Innovative Multi-Stakeholder Partnerships for Urban Basic Services. Examined emerging business models reshaping urban basic service delivery — including guarantee-based financing mechanisms, private sector participation frameworks and PPP models. Framed pathways to formalise collaboration through MoUs and Letters of Intent, aligned with UN-Habitat's Strategic Plan 2026–2029. Target audience: national governments, local authorities, private sector actors, utilities and DFIs.
Session 3 — South–South Cooperation for Transformative Urban Development
Thematic Dialogue on South–South Cooperation (SSC) for Transformative Urban Development. Developed the strategic case for SSC as a structured framework for urban cooperation — moving beyond ad hoc exchanges toward formalised peer learning, joint programming and triangular cooperation. Identified 3–4 priority thematic areas and explored a concrete post-WUF13 cooperation product (MoA/MoU) with UNOSSC. Target audience: Ministers, national urban focal points, Permanent Missions, UNOSSC leadership and DFIs engaged in SSC.
